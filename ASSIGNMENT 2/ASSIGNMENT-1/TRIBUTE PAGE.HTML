About Steve Jobs

Steve Paul Jobs, born in 1995 and died in 2011 was a tech visionary entrepreneur who is the co-founder of Apple company. He played a pivotal role in revolutionizing personal computing and mobile devices.

KEY ARCHIEVEMENTS & EDUCATION

  • He attended Reed College in 1972 before withdrawing that same year. In 1974, he traveled through India, seeking enlightenment before later studying Zen Buddhism. He and Wozniak co-founded
  • Jobs initiated the personal computer revolution with the Apple II and Macintosh, the first mass-produced personal computers with a mouse and graphical user interface, released in 1977 and 1984 respectively.
  • Jobs revolutionized personal computer interaction by replacing text-based commands with a mouse-driven graphical user interface, borrowing the idea from Xerox's Palo Alto Research Center, which failed to recognize its potential.
  • In 1985, Jobs departed Apple after a long power struggle with the company's board and its then-CEO, John Sculley. That same year, Jobs took some Apple employees with him to found NeXT, a computer platform development company that specialized in computers for higher-education and business markets, serving as its CEO.
  • In 1997, Jobs returned to Apple as CEO after the company's acquisition of NeXT. He was largely responsible for reviving Apple, which was on the verge of bankruptcy.
  • In 1986, he bought the computer graphics division of Lucasfilm, which was spun off independently as Pixar.[3] Pixar produced the first computer-animated feature film, Toy Story (1995), and became a leading animation studio, producing dozens of commercially successful and critically acclaimed films.
